# HR-Employee-Attrition-Dashboard-Excel-Project-
This project analyzes employee attrition data to uncover patterns and drivers of employee turnover.
It provides HR teams with a clear, data-driven overview of key workforce metrics, helping them make informed decisions to improve retention and job satisfaction.

**Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset (1,470 employee records)
Key Fields: Department, Age, Job Role, Income, Years at Company, Job Satisfaction, Overtime, Attrition, etc.

**Tools Used:**
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts
Conditional Formatting
Slicers for interactivity
KPI cards and dynamic summaries

**Key Metrics:**
Total Employees: 1,470
Attrition Rate: 16%
Average Monthly Income: $6,502.93
Average Tenure: 2.8 years
Average Job Satisfaction: 2.7/4

**Insights:**
Highest attrition in the Research and Development department
Mid-aged employees (31–45) and those with 2–4 years tenure most likely to leave
Overtime workers are more prone to attrition
Low-income earners have higher turnover rates
Higher satisfaction correlates with retention

**Business Recommendation:**
Review compensation structure for Research and Development and low-income employees.
Implement work-life balance initiatives to reduce overtime attrition.
Target mid-tenure employees with career growth programs to improve retention.

**Deliverables:**
Clean HR dataset
Interactive Excel Dashboard
Summary insights sheet
